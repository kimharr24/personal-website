Recent
Projects

The rock paper scissors project harnesses the power of computer vision by training a neural network to recognize your selections in real time. With most of the details hidden away, users only need to execute a few lines to automatically generate a dataset using their hand, train a convolutional network, and then have it predict your hand movements. This project is still in progress.


Human Activity Recognition (HAR), the use of sensor data to identify activities performed by an individual, suffers from a lack of data. To address this issue, my research at Worcester Polytechnic Institute led to the proposal of a novel Controllable Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) capable of generating synthetic data, improving upon state-of-the-art HAR GANs by up to 10%. Read more in our paper above.
